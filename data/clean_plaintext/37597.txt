Alwhyle ye drynke, 'midst age and ache ybent,
Ah creep not comfortlesse besyde our streame;
(Sweete nurse of hope) afflyction's downwarde sente,
Wythe styll smalle voyce, to rouze from thryftlesse dreame;
Each wyng to prune, that shiftythe everie spraie.
In wytelesse flyghte, and chyrpythe lyfe awaie.

Alwhyle ye lave &mdash; suche solace may be founde
"When kynde the hande, why 'neath its healynge faynte?"
"Payne shall recure, the heartes corruptede wounde,"
"Farre gone is that, whych feelethe not its playnte."
"Ay kyndrede angel smote, Betheseda gave"
"Newe vyrtues forthe, &mdash; and felt her troublede wave."

Thus drynke, thus lave &mdash; nor evermore lamente;
Oure sprynges but flowe pale anguish to befriende;
Howe fayre the meed that followe the contente!
How bleste to live, and fynde suche anguish mende!
How bleste to dye, when sufferynge faithe makes sure,
At lyfe's high founte, an everlastynge cure!
